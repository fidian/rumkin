What is this thing?

Pick your favorite:

  • It is just a get-together of friends that happens every year. A great excuse to see each other and swap stories.
  • We hate trees and devote our lives to making them burn.
  • Where else are you encouraged to play with fire?
  • Party all night!

Besides, what better thing do you have to do that weekend?

There's a rumor that it is for someone's birthday party, but there's no October birthdays of which I am aware.

Need to know more? I have a category in my photo album devoted to this thing, and there's even a video of Jeff blowing short shots of fire (avi format).


What is Provided?

We will provide the usual items:

  • Many hot dogs (both the no-skin type and the old fashioned skin-on style) with enough buns
  • At least 1 big package of big marshmallows
  • Some pop (likely a couple 2-liter bottles of Coke, Mountain Dew, etc.)
  • Grenadine (for making Cherry Coke, Kiddie Cocktails, Ülli's "grenadine and a splash of pop" drink, etc.)
  • Chips (not potato chips, but unknown type and quantity)
  • Ketchup, Mustard, Relish (Dill if possible)
  • Fire-friendly paper cups (For the "Who's is this?" "Dunno" "Toss it in the fire" type of conversation)

In addition, I will likely be bringing a bottle of rum for mixing rum & Cokes. If you have some bottles of booze gathering dust in your cupboard, just bring it and we'll see what happens.

Breakfast this year will be eggs, bacon, toast, milk, etc. Be hungry. The last couple years, not enough breakfast was served. In fact, nobody was flopping on the floor, begging that we "just end it all" because they ate too much. We must change that this year. (2 doz eggs or more, 4lbs bacon or more, 2 loaves bread, can you eat it all?)


What should you bring?

If you are rich, large donations (eg. $1,250.75) are accepted, but only if you were just going to buy a small country, fund a politician, or otherwise waste the money. If you have a special desire to eat turkey hotdogs or have muffins for breakfast, feel free to bring them. However, this isn't like a potluck dinner -- you aren't required to bring food/drinks unless you really don't like what is being served. If that is the case, just call me before hand and maybe I can change the menu. Regarding alcohol, bring whatever you want and it is sometimes nice to share. Lots-o-fun!

For staying the night, you may wish to bring pillows, blankets, a towel (shower is definately available). Unfortunately, the only place to sleep is on the floor in my parent's house or outside. Fortunately, they just got new carpeting in 2002, so it should still be fluffy (or stiff from the starch). If you have a tent, sleeping bags, or you just like to freeze to death under the stars, that works for me. If you have a tent to set up, come earlier because the light from that big ball of fire in the sky will assist you. Heck, I have tents, cots, and sleeping bags to share, if someone wants to go that route. Just let me know in advance!
