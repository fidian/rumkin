User Rating: / 0

This is a list of passwords most commonly seen on past years, sorted for decreasing number of occurrences (that is, more common passwords are listed first).
The password trends show that the average user cares less about choosing a strong password and more about memo-ability.

Our life these days is largely dependent on passwords whether shopping online, transferring funds or sending emails passwords have a part to play.

With fraudsters more and more turning online to get money and we all hear about security breaches and fraud stories all the time.

It still amazes us how people think of passwords as a word they can remember easy, it might just be time for a change if your password is listed here.

Your online accounts, computer files, and personal information are more secure when you use strong passwords to help protect them.

Do you use strong passwords?
A strong password should appear to be a random string of characters to an attacker. It should be 14 characters or longer, (eight characters or longer at a minimum). It should include a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
